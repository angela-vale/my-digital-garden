Yesterday, I thought everything was going to be ok. Tonight, I don't know if we will be able to keep together.

It started this morning. I wantted to know about [[Elin\|Elin]], so I approached [[Ilsevel\|Ilsevel]] while we were packing up. I asked about Elin, making some excuse that I had heard of her. 

Elin is her daughter, her daughter that died 20 years ago.

The news sent me reeling. Dead? Do I have a ghost in my head? I thought I might be able to meet her, speak to her, but now... I tried not to think of it before, that the voice was just some quirk of intuition. Knowing she was real, I thought I would be able to prove to myself that I wasn't mad, that no one like me was mad, but now...

I didn't handle the news well, and I fear I made Ilsevel more upset than I intended. I made up a story that I had read letters by Elin, letters I had found in the Library. Thallen looked suspicious, but didn't say anything. I even promised Ilsevel I would send her some of the documents, so she could have something to remember her daughter by. I will need to find some way to make that happen. 

But, that is for later, when we can return. If we would even be welcomed back...

[[Berry Boy\|Berry Boy]] took us to [[the Bridge\|the Bridge]]. It was magnificent, red stone, three or four stories high, with tents and campers overflowing on both sides of the river. On the other side, the wedding festivities were still loud, despite how early it was.

[[LLK/Belnar\|Belnar]] left us, having errands to run. The rest of us separated.  Thallen and Capella to send letters home, Fen to meet a contact, and I decided to find any information about [[Attan\|Attan]].

I found an elf named [[Nana\|Nana]], a refugee from Attan.  Her news was dire. The [[Court of the Monarch\|Court of the Monarch]] has been corrupted and itoo dangerous to approach. The plants and animals are dying, and it's spreading out from the Court like a slow plague. And worse, there are creatures roaming, creatures ade out of dead plants. She, and others, left to try to get help, something the people of the forest rarely do. I told her we would help, that we were heading there.

As I turned to leave, she told me [[Arumanus\|Arumanus]]'s last son is dead.

As I returned to our meeting place, a shout rose up from the party. A barge was coming down the river, a hydra head on it. [[Andraste\|Andraste]]'s hunt was a success.

Once we had all returned, Fen shared some disturbing news: his contact didn't recognise him. It wasn't a ploy, that he could tell, and she didn't show signs of playing a joke or being a doppleganger or the like. She treated him like she had never met him before.

It brought to mind the member of the [[Stars Eternal\|Stars Eternal]] who had never heard of Thallen or Capella, or anyone else who knew her name. Thallen resisted the thought, but I knew he was right. Belnar did something.

Everyone but me has met [[Maywin\|Maywin]], so, the next logical step was to get second confirmation. We asked Berry Boy to bring us to her. He brought us onto the bridge until he passage ways grew too small for him. Luckily, we were close and we were able to find her in a room nearby.

Maywin welcomed us into the room, recognising us as the group from her vision. When pressed, she didn't remember us at all. Even the hunt she had gone on with Fen, she recounted the details, but without him, though she mentioned a wolf that she could not place how he came to be helping her. It's as confirmed as it can be: she, too, did not recognise us. Now, even Thallen could not contest it.

With her were others. [[Lauryl of Inara\|Lauryl of Inara]], a stone child from [[LLK/Fort Calagal\|Fort Calagal]], [[Sarta\|Sarta]], an elf with bluish skin from the border of the [[LLK/Feylands\|Feylands]], and [[Wilmer of Ochre Town\|Wilmer of Ochre Town]], a town on the shores of the seas [[LLK/The Hirthingi\|The Hirthingi]] raid.

Wilmer and Sarta told us that [[LLK/Lavinia\|Lavinia]] was at [[Fort Sitern\|Fort Sitern]] with [[Alais (temp)\|Alais (temp)]], their leader. Wilmer diliked her greatly and hypothesised that she had been sent away from Rovanna as she had no more power.  Both he and Sarta had gone to Fort Sitern looking for help, and both were sent to the Bridge. The Feylands are expanding, ad the Hirthingi are pushing for more territory. It's perilous, and they need help.

And Lauryl, too, she came to seek help to fight the demons in the south. All of this danger since Tau left. All of this chaos, because Tau left? Or did Tau leave because this chaos was coming? Tau needs Urion to do something, was he holding off all of this chaos?

We decided we needed to talk to Belnar before we could move forward. Capella wanted to go to the wedding with Wilmer, so Fen and I joined her while Thallen went to gather Belnar.

Andraste was there, with her brother and his groom. They have a lovely custom here, where you challenge one of the happy couple to a competition. If they lose, then thier parnter says "I still think you are the best, and I love you." It's heart warming to see. And the Hydra meat was wonderful. 

Capella challenged [[Ancasta\|Ancasta]] to a singing contest, which she won handily, and then beat Andraste in a foot race. SHe praised Capella, though said she should challenge her again when she is sober.

Suddenly, Fen said we had to go, Ard was in a panic. We ran and found Thallen on their knees, despondent.  Belnar had decided we were no longer worth his time and had dismissed us. 

Im not sure who was more upset, Fen or Capella. We followed Ard through the crowds until we found Belnar, siting in one of the Bridge's plazas under the dwarven statue. She was not amused to see us, told us that we had failed, that we were unworthy to assist them. They had found new followers who wouldn't delay or question.

She confirmed that it was she who made everyone forget us as our mission is too secret. SHe dropped everything we gave her, and we pushed, trying to get answers. How do we reverse it? What are we supposed to do now that we have been cut off?

Frustrated and angry that we wouldn't leave them be and accept their decree, they let it out.

Thallen has a parasitic curse.

Fen's father is Urion's worst enemy.

Capella is useless and spoiled.

And I...I am a sorcerer.

I deflected, of course, turning to ask Fen about his father when he questioned me. He doesn't know his father, has no idea who he is, and we got caught up in that enough that we didn't return to me for a time. 

But Fen hasn't forgotten, and neither has Thallen. I expect I will be confronted in the morning. With no one remembering us, then I can't say the Vilento will vouch for me.

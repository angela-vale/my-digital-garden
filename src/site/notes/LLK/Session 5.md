The Feylands were almost incomprehensible. From where we stood, trees seemed to shoot out in all directions, and yet you could walk on any of them as if they were on the ground. And what trees! Made of plant, some, but others of some mineral, or glass or stone, shaped like trees and with the feel of bark under my hand, but not at all like any tree I have ever seen before.

[[LLK/Ard\|Ard]] was joyous, of course, to be back in his home land, but we were confused. And a little nauseous. Eventually, Fen was able to communicate our desire to speak to someone who could speak to us in return and provide the promised help.

Ard ran off, sometimes seeming to run on a wall or upside-down, but moving with such ease that it didn't feel unnatural. He returned with a [[feyling,\|feyling,]] [[Katla\|Katla]]. She looked elven, but there was something otherworldly about her, something ageless and wise, but also...childlike. Capella and Katla hit it off right away.

Katla explained so much in her rambling way. We are dead, spirits without bodies. There is a ghost in the [[Great Hall\|Great Hall]]. We could get new bodies if we were to go to this place called [[Haven\|Haven]], but we had a purpose to get [[LLK/Tau\|Tau]] to return, so that plants can grow again, so that the world will not die.

Katla said the world does not need the gods. Tau does not make the plants grow, they don't need the sun. They grow through something called [[Telluric energy\|Telluric energy]], an energy of the heart. 

I have never heard of such a thing, and now I regret not bringing more reference books with me because I need to know if such a thing could be true. I wonder how much Vilento knows...

We needed to move ahead, and it was clear that the fey and feylings would not be able to give us the answers we sought. We could only talk to Katla as she has some special ability. In the Feyling village, outside of the Feylands proper, we were ghosts, unable to see beyond the Veil into the world of the living.

Ard satisfied, and the rest of us now sure that we would get no more answers, we decided to go to the Divine Realm and try to see Urion. Thallen and Capella were both eager, so we went.

We ran back through the darkness, Ard leading the way, this time without the lantern.  Katla said such a thing only allowed the gods to control us and bring us where they wished. 

We passed [[Ninshar\|Ninshar]], the gate keeper, and his avian attendants without stopping, and entered the Divine Realm.

Tau's presence was overwhelming when we arrived, almost stifling, but, before we could go far, [[LLK/Belet Anzu\|Belet Anzu]] grabbed hold of us and we were at once in a large marble vault, huge and cavernous, with pools in the middle. [[Kigal\|Kigal]] was there, weeping with some of her attendants.

Belat Anzu told Kigal that she was to host us until he returned, and then he vanished. She made us feel welcomed, but would not tell us why she was crying. Her reticence reminded me of [[Lilal Rammi\|Lilal Rammi]]'s, some secret the gods had to keep from us mortals.

Capella was happy to see her, as Kigal was the god who directed Urion to save her as an infant. She sang, and [[Ell\|Ell]] came and listened. It was a wonderful song, so sweet and powerful, that it eased Kigal's tears for a time and she answered some questions.

Tau is with Urion, and will remain there until Urion does as Tau wishes. Tau will have her way, and will see the continuation of her [[Grand Design\|Grand Design]]. And what she wants, is for Urion to continue.

But, if that were so, why would Tau have agreed to allow Urion to attempt to raise Vanna? Why would she have claimed his soul or allowed him to die at all if she wanted him to continue his mission?

We are resting now. Ard is swimming, and Fen seems to have found some food. We wait until Belat Anzu returns. Apparently, he has something he wishes us to do. But what could a god want with us?

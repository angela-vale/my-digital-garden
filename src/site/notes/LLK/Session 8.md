We came upon the group after some hours of walking. Fen was asleep by the fire, apparently having over indulged. Not that I hold it against him. He ran here much more quickly than we did. 

I took the lead, introducing our group to them. When they asked which gods we follow, I said Tau, of course. When questioned why I said ‘of course’, I told them that Tau was the patron of Rosanna, so who better to follow?

They accepted that answer, though I could tell they were partially distracted by Capellla. I feel a fool for not thinking of it before. Tieflings are feared and mistrusted throughout the land, sometimes considered curse. I was so distracted by my own worry of causing tension with the nomads that I didn’t think of other worries. 

It did not help that they quickly came to the conclusion that she was…stupid. She isn’t, not really. Naive, yes, sheltered certainly, not wise to the ways of the world, of course. She has been surrounded by her father’s people for her whole life, she never suffered from any real prejudice. I should talk to her, let her know what is to likely to come as we continue travelling. Protecting her innocence is not worth the risk her naivety may put her in. 

The travellers put us at a separate fire, clearly uneasy around us. I tried to show goodwill, sharing some of the food we caught and prepared with them, offering to stand watch while they rested, but they said the only suspicious thing here was us. 

And so, the chance at trading something with them faded away. 

After we ate, Capella wanted to pet the horses, especially the unicorn. Concerned and wary to make sure that no one cause any more trouble, I accompanied her. Also, a real live unicorn. How could I say no to seeing one up close?

Capella acted as if she never seen a horse before and had never ridden. I hadn’t even considered that a possibility. I had assumed she knew how to ride, but when had she ever had to leave the palace?

The man who showed us the horses was kind enough, if a little tense, and the young strix seemed to actually like us. 

At the least, they showed us where we were in the map and they told us where the bridge to cross the river is. There will likely be people there, gathered for the crossing, traders and the like. We will need to go there, gathering what we can along the way that may be of value so we can trade. I may need to brush up on some old habits and make something out of nothing. 

When we returned to the fire, Capella sang. The nomads then understood why we were travelling with someone so unfit for it. 

They left sometime afterwards, not friendly but not overtly upset with us. We rested a while longer before we left. 

Elin once travelled through these lands. She has been speaking now more than in a long while, talking about her journeys, where she had found food, various landmarks. Because of her, I have found potatoes and little bunches of trees and bushes where there was game and berries. 

I’m not mad. She is real. These last few days prove that if nothing else did. I have tried to ignore her for many years, most of my life, but she is actually giving important information now, useful hints to help us survive. I wish Vilento was here. Maybe he could provide some insight. 

Although, Vilento could do nothing for Thallen. Belat Anzu told us that Thallen is cursed. Not the details, of course, because he rarely says more than he must at any given time. I told Thallen that if they wished to share, I would be happy to take a look. After all, whatever this curse is, it clearly means they cannot be near us, and that could be most inconvenient if it were to act up. Given we have been returned only a few days and it has already pushed him away twice, I worry this may cause more trouble for us. 

We travelled another day through the darkness following the river. According to the nomads, we should be another day to the River, and from there, another two or three to the Crossing.

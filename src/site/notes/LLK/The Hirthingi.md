The Hirthingi were among the first mortals. They were navigators from the start, created and educated by three of the most important gods at the time: Essem of the sea, Neshka the warrior and Ametek the clever. They were made to be the "older siblings" of mortals, taking care of all the other people that were slowly coming into existence around the sea, and further south. 

They're not on the map but there are rivers going across the land from the sea so they would be able to visit everyone, distribute resources as required, etc. 

But they were also the first to be abandoned by the gods, as Essem, Neshka & Ametek all left after less than two centuries had passed. The Hirthingi didn't take this well at all. They weren't left to fend for themselves, other gods would still visit and teach them things, but certainly their privileged position was lost, and soon the places that still had their patron gods surpassed them. 

Eventually as almost all the gods abandoned mortals, they decided to fully turn their backs on them and stop any form of worship. They tried to fully forbid divine magic for a while but that didn't end up well, now it's merely very taboo and regulated. Arcane magic is seen very positively on the other hand, since it allows one to be powerful independently of the gods; and so they have a lot of little enclaves that study & try to develop new forms of magic... with varying degrees of success. Sorcery is seen poorly everywhere, but especially so here as it's perceived as divine in origins.

Because of their contempt & hostility towards the gods, they have a hard time maintaining relations with other tribes. This has led to them being more isolationists, and, consequently, to depend on raids to procure resources unavailable on their islands -- although they also use the raids to further their anti religionism, sacking temples and such.

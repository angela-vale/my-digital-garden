Death is not what I expected. 

Lilial Rammi came, as I expected, to guide us to the Golden Vault, but instead, she lead us to the volcano and left us to be attacked by a demon. 

We fought back, barely able to hold our own against the blackened and burnt creature. Elin said this is not usual, that something must be wrong with Maarna for a demon to be in this state, but what could be wrong, I did not hear. 

The demon fled when [[LLK/Belet Anzu\|Belet Anzu]] appeared, wearing the face of the king though much too tall and with a mighty sword on his back.  He was angry, at Thallen, at us, at [[Lilal Rammi \|Lilal Rammi ]]for leading us astray. 

Something is wrong with the gods, something more than Tau not returning. I have never heard of the dead being given a choice of where to go, and I have never heard of a god as high as Belat Anzu coming to collect the souls of the dead. Vilento warned me that something was amiss with the gods, but I never expected it to be as fractured as this. 

So, who to go with, Belat Anzu who said he would bring us to our king? Lilat Rammi, who against Belat Anzu’s wishes, told us she would take us to Inarra? I have never doubted the gods before, but I did then. They cannot lie, so far as we know, but that does not mean they speak the whole truth. Did he wear Urion’s face to make us trust him?

Fen, or I should say Ard, offered an alternative: the Feylands. We have friends among the Fey, he said. 

Thallen, of course, wanted to go with Belat Anzu, and Capella wanted to see her father. Feb seemed more than happy to trust Ard, and I, for the first time, did not think I could trust one of the gods. 

Before we could come to a conclusion, Ard leapt to Belat Anzu, growing horns like a stag from his wolfish head, and snagged the lantern from his hands, and then he ran. 

I grabbed Capella and gave chase, for Lilal Rammi warned us it was dangerous to leave its light and I knew in my bones that was true. The others followed and we ran for hours through darkness, no wind on our faces, no landscape to mark our movements, nothing at all but a steady movement of our feet until, at last, Ard stopped. 

We had arrived. 

The Feylands is a world of life, not the afterlife. We drank poison, but we did not pass into the divine realm. Are we dead? Truly dead? Or are we phantoms, ghosts looking to solve our unfinished business?

Have we made a terrible mistake? Only time will tell. 

- an excerpt from the journal of Sigrid Guthrunsdottir

We stayed for a long time in Kagali's home.  I couldn't say how long, as time moves and feels differently since we died. It may have been weeks since that day we were sacrificed, or it could be hours. 

Kagali was a good host, bringing in whatever we wanted and providing food, though I"m ot sure I have felt actual hunger since I died. She also asked us to share happy memories with her, something to ease her sadness.

Thallen shared about their family, merchants in the city of Rovanna. They talked about working in the shop, about spending time with them doing the normal daily tasks of life. It made me wonder what my life could have been like if my family had been able to live a normal life...

I shared the day I entered the capital and passed the exams to become an apprentice in the palace. I was so happy to pass, so happy to find a place I could belong, happy to not have been discovered. 

Capella shared how she learned her first instrument, the flute, and the time she spent with King Urion. While I knew Urion, it was always at arm's length, not personal. To hear Capella speak to him not as a king but as a man, as a father, it helped me to understand him better. And her desire to see him again. 

Fen shared the tale of a long and difficult hunt for a fugitive that resulted in him finding a very shiny button that he added to his horde. Fen is...the strangest man I have ever met. I must be sure to keep anything shiny that I value away from him lest he attempt to claim it in some (hopefully non-lethal) compitition.

Ard shared something as wel, barking and woofing and acting out some tale, but no one could understanding him except Kagali, who seemed very amused, clapping and giggling enough to forget her tears. I must attempt to find a spell so I can understand Ard without needing to rely on a translator...

Eventially, [[LLK/Belet Anzu\|Belet Anzu]] returned, though this time he appeared in a form more like Kagali, though flatter, somehow. I think he lacks the imagination to do anything with his hair. No flair. Or maybe he is just tired.

He transported us to a field of smouldering ash, the sky blocked by thick, dark clouds of smoke. This, he said, was what was before Tau's Design and would be again after. And this would be Rovanna if we did not do what he needed us to do.

And what he needed us to do was on the mortal realm.

We woke in a field, darkness all around us, no Tau or Ibbal above us. In the distance, faintly glowing with in the distance. The [[Great Hall\|Great Hall]].

I was me again. Or, rather, I was aware of my body again. Of breathing, hunger, the feeling of wind on my skin. Even Ref felt like a weight on my shoulder again. I was alive, but there was something different. I felt different.

Belat Anzu was there, but now appearing as an elf man. There was no aura around him. He was in a vessel. He had given us new bodies, with slightly different abilities. Our mission was to go to [[Attan\|Attan]].

Thallen kept his distance, claiming that he was sick and didn't want us to catch it. That makes no sense, because why would Belat Anzu make his new body sick? Belat Anzu said he was broken, just like he had before when we first met in the dark, and Thallen's soul was flickering. Thallen used to visit Vilento, and he confirmed it was the same affliction, that if my master could not cure them, how could I? I suppose he does not know that I have different abilities than my master.

We travelled for a short time before we realized our hunger was too much and we made camp.  Fen and Thallen went hunting, Capella and I gathered wood to make a fire. They returned with a deer and two rabbits. One rabbit, Thallen gave to us while they kept the other and made their own fire. Fen dug a pit to cook the deer in and we sat down to relax...

...And then the Kobolds showed up. They had scented the meat and wanted it for themselves.  We defended ourselves amiably, adapting to our slightly different abilities well. 

We gave Belat Anzu one of the kobold swords and Capella taught him the nuances of using a blade ("Grab and Stab") after we ate. 

I attempted to freeze the leftover meat. It was how we kept our meat good during the long winters when game was less plenty when i was growing up, but I have never attempted it by magic and, sadly, it failed.  We'll have to do more hunting tomorrow, and we will have to find more supplies. 

Belat Anzu doesn't know how far we are from Attan, and, except for Fen, we are not equipped for long travel. Bed rolls, water skins, even a cooking pot. I didn't bring any gold with me, after all, the dead don't need money. I have my cloak, at least, which I can sleep on, and Thallen is a soldier. They have travelled on campaign before. But, Capella is unprepared for the hardships of long journeys on foot.

Fearing more kobolds, or something else, I have taken the first watch. When my hours are done, I will wake Fen. Ard is still awake, though as a fae, I'm not sure if he needs sleep. Thallen has chosen to sleep apart from the rest of us for his own reasons, still claiming he does not wish to make us ill. 

But, what could Belat Anzu wish for us to do, and what does it have to do with the Court of the Monarch? If he needed some information, why would ha need us to go and not just go himself?

Ibbal is rising, and the planes are quiet. I will be able to rest soon.

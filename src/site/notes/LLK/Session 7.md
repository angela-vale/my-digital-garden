We woke in the middle of the night to Thallen screaming. 

It seems that Fen, in order not to approach Thallen due to his malady, tossed a rock to wake him up. The rock hit his armour, which made Thallen think he was under attack. 

We went back to sleep, only to be woken up a few hours later when we were actually attacked. By Kobolds. Again. 

Fen and Ard dispatched a few quickly, Thallen got cuts on his legs, and Belat Anzu was hurt. Capella insisted we give in to their demands and give them the uneaten part of our deer from last night. As we were “negotiating” our safe passage, a dragon appeared from underground. 

It wasn’t a large dragon, probably not even an adolescent, but none of us have fought a dragon before, not even Thallen, and if our princess says to make peace, we will do our best. 

While it was tense, we managed to drag the deer to the dragon without much issue and backed away. They withdrew and we walked away, little worse for the wear though without the boon of a future meal preserved. 

To make matters worse, while we can heal ourselves , at least a bit, Belat Anzu cannot. Fen made some herbal remedy and smeared it on Belat Anzu’s leg, but it will take time before he can walk long distances on it. 

I worry that this will not be an easy trip to the Amat forest. Except for Fen, we are not well equipped for such a journey, and we have little of value to trade even if we do find someone willing. Belat Anzu could have deposited us somewhat closer to our destination, or at least provided some supplies. At this rate, we may need to trade our weapons for food. 

Ref and Ard went scouting for any sign of danger or a route ahead, or any friendly folk. Ard returned with news of a group travelling south on horseback. 

Thallen, though injured, carried Belat Anzu and Fen ran ahead to meet them in hopes of catching up to them and seeing if they would trade with us. 

I am a little wary of meeting with the people of the Valley. For one, the Hirthingi have raided in these lands for time immemorial, and traded. Beyond that, the Hirthingi don’t follow gods and the nomads of the Valley are fiercely loyal to their gods, though the left long ago. There is much mutual distrust. 

While I no longer have the accent and am half elf, my name is clearly Hirthingi. I will need to take care not to arrouse their suspicion or ire. 

-

Fen catches up with the group and makes tentative friends. They share food and Fen sits by their fire. He learns that they are off to hunt a hydra to gift a head to their leaders brother for his wedding. They also mark the map Fen carries with sites of known dangers and dragons to aid the group through their travel.
